Boat Anatomy — Close-Up Observations

Understanding the hull, planking, keel, and stem lets you read a boat's history. Close-up photos show paint layers, waterlines, and load marks.

  • Keel & stem — structural spine and bow shaping.
  • Paint & antifouling — reveals maintenance cadence.
  • Hardware — chain, cleats, and fittings show age and usage.

Quick Tips

How to photograph boat close-ups:

  1. Use a macro or 50mm lens for texture.
  2. Shoot golden hour for soft reflections.
  3. Focus on geometry — numbers, rope lines, and portholes.
